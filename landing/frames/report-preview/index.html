03 · The Physio Report

  • This is the feature. Everything else — the niggle tracking, the shoe logging, the effort ratings — was groundwork. This report is where all that quiet data collection pays off. It's the moment the runner thinks: "I'm glad I logged all that."
  • The physio's first 30 seconds: Summary stats → niggle timeline → trend flags. That's the diagnostic flow. Total load context, injury pattern, and what's notable. A physio can scan this in under a minute and already have more information than 15 minutes of questioning would provide.
  • Niggle timeline is chronological, not grouped. Physios think in time — "when did this start, how did it progress?" Grouping by body part would hide temporal relationships. The timeline lets them see: hamstring first appeared Mar 18, escalated through three runs, and hit moderate by Apr 10.
  • The run log table gives biomechanical context the physio can cross-reference. "The moderate hamstring flag was during intervals at 4:02/km, effort 8/10, in a shoe with 342km" — that's three data points a physio would normally have to extract through conversation.
  • Session-type colour dots in the run log aren't decoration. They let the physio quickly scan for patterns: "Are niggles clustering around a specific session type?" In this example, niggles appear across easy, long, and interval sessions — telling the physio this isn't purely an intensity issue.
  • Shoe rotation section serves two purposes. First: mileage correlation with injury ("342km shoe, 3 of 4 hamstring issues"). Second: rotation pattern analysis ("speed work always in the worn shoe, easy in the fresh one" — is that contributing?).
  • Trend flags are the killer feature. These are the patterns that are nearly impossible to spot in conversation. A human can't easily track that weekly load jumped 18% in the same week the first niggle appeared, or that 3/4 hamstring issues were in the same shoe. Steady sees these because it has the structured data. The physio couldn't ask the right questions without knowing the answers first.
  • "Correlation noted — not necessarily causal" is deliberate language. The report positions Steady as an informed observer, not a diagnostic tool. It raises the questions; the physio answers them. This builds trust with both runners and physio professionals.
  • For the landing page: This is the hero image. Show the report, not the app. The message: "Every run you log builds towards a moment like this. When you walk into your physio appointment, you don't have to remember anything — Steady already did."
