Steady is a free training plan manager for runners who already know what they're doing. Built by a runner who got tired of living in spreadsheets. Bring your Pfitz, your Hansons, your coach's block. We help you execute it.

Why I built this
I was managing my marathon plan across a Google Sheet, the Notes app, Strava screenshots, and a ChatGPT window that forgot everything every session. Four tools. None of them talked to each other. So I built the tool I actually wanted.
The problem

Your plan lives in a spreadsheet. Your runs live in Strava. Nothing connects them.

You built a plan — from Pfitzinger, from Jack Daniels, from an LLM conversation. Now you're tracking planned vs actual in a Google Sheet you made at 11pm. When something goes wrong — fatigue, a missed session, a pace that felt too hard — you re-open ChatGPT, re-explain your entire training history, and hope it remembers what happened last week. It doesn't.

Steady fixes this. One place for your plan, your runs, and the gap between them.

Your plan, your way

Bring your own plan. We don't generate cookie-cutter blocks.

You bring the plan you trust — from a book, a coach, or an LLM. Steady structures it into weeks and phases, tracks it against your actual runs from Strava, and shows you what happened vs what was supposed to happen.

Free. No plan lock-in. No subscription required.

The full picture

Planned vs actual. Per session. Per week. Per block.

See your entire training cycle — BASE, BUILD, PEAK, TAPER — laid out with volume tracking, session type dots, and adherence bars. Know exactly where you are in the plan and what's coming next.

No spreadsheet formulas. No manual entry. Just sync your watch and look.

Optional paid add-on

A coach that reads your splits and actually remembers last week.

Steady's AI coach analyses your sessions, flags fatigue patterns, and proposes specific plan changes — not generic advice. It initiates conversations after your runs, not the other way around.

This is an optional add-on. The app works perfectly without it. But if you want a second pair of eyes on your training, it's there.
