Why I built this
Hey, I'm Cyprian! I'm a Product Manager and a runner, more of a nerdy one. During race prep I was managing my training plan across a few different tools. When I got injured mid-block and I didn't really have a way of tracking my recovery or adapting the plan. So I decided to try and build something I would actually want to use.
The problem

Your plan lives in one app, your runs live somewhere else. When something goes wrong, you can't adapt.

Outside of AI-created running apps most runners manage their plans through spreadsheets, a watch app, and some with an AI chat to stitch it together. Life happens, fatigue and injuries are real and rarely do you stick 100% to your original plan. Steady puts the plan and the runs in the same place so the full picture is always there.

Your plan, your way

Bring your own plan in whatever shape or form.

Use the builder for a clean setup. Upload a CSV if your plan lives in a sheet. Or drop in a screenshot and let Steady turn it into something you can actually use.

Sync runs from Garmin, Apple Watch, or Strava so your plan and your real training finally sit in the same place.

When things go wrong

Injuries will happen, the key is to adapt.

Mark the injury, log what you can do while you're out, and follow a return-to-running progression as you come back. The remaining block adapts around the time you've lost.

Your goal gets reassessed too. If you've lost three weeks, Steady is honest that your target might need revisiting. But if you come back ahead of schedule, you can reset it.

No other app treats injury as a first-class part of training. This is one of the main reasons Steady exists.

Block view

See exactly where you are in the block.

The current phase stays front and centre, while the rest of the block fades back. You can scan what you've done, what's left, and how each week is stacking up against the plan.

Real coaching, not a chatbot

Invite your coach into the plan.

Your real human coach can see everything - every session, every planned vs actual gap - and make changes to the plan directly. No spreadsheets over email. No re-explaining the block before every call. The coach adjusts what's next based on how you're actually performing.

Optional paid add-on

Steady AI reads the full block, not just today's run.

After key sessions, Steady AI reads the full training block and gives feedback in context. It spots fatigue patterns, suggests plan changes when things drift, and initiates the conversation proactively.

This is the only paid feature. It exists to cover LLM costs and should never feel like it's being pushed.

WHO IT'S FOR

Built for runners who already have a plan.

You already have a plan - from a coach, a book, or one you built yourself. You want to track it properly, handle the weeks where things go sideways, and not switch to an app that replaces your plan with its own. That's the gap Steady fills.
