You have a plan.
Now you need a tool that actually follows it.

Steady is a free training plan manager for runners who already know what they're doing. Built by a runner who got tired of juggling spreadsheets, screenshots, and disconnected apps. Bring your Pfitz, your Jack Daniels plan, or your coach's block. We help you follow it properly.

I promise no spam - it's only to gather interest and give you a heads up when we launch.

Why I built this
I was managing my running plans across Google Sheets, the Notes app, Strava, Apple Watch and Garmin screenshots, and AI that kept losing context every time I added more screenshots. Too many tools. No integrations between them. So I built the tool I actually wanted for myself.
The problem

Your plan lives in one place. Your runs live somewhere else. The story keeps getting lost.

Maybe your plan came from Pfitzinger. Maybe Jack Daniels. Maybe a coach. Maybe an LLM conversation that actually gave you something useful. Either way, once training gets messy, you're back in a spreadsheet, your notes, and a chat window trying to stitch everything back together.

When fatigue creeps in, you miss a session, or a workout feels way harder than it should, you have to re-explain the whole block from scratch. That's the part that feels broken.

Steady fixes this. One place for your plan, your runs, and the gap between them.

Your plan, your way

Bring your plan in however you already have it.

Use the builder if you want a clean setup. Upload a CSV if your plan already lives in a sheet. Or drop in a screenshot or photo of your current plan and let Steady turn it into something you can actually use.

Then sync the running data you already have from Garmin, Apple Watch, or Strava so your plan and your real training finally sit in the same place.

Free. No plan lock-in. No subscription required.

Block view

See exactly where you are in the block.

The current phase stays front and centre, while the rest of the block fades back. You can scan what you've done, what's left, and how each week is stacking up against the plan without decoding a spreadsheet first.

It gives you the simple answers you actually want mid-block: what phase am I in, how much have I done, and what's next?

Optional paid add-on

Steady Coach reads the full block, not just today's run.

Steady Coach is the optional paid add-on. It looks at your sessions, spots fatigue patterns, and suggests actual plan changes when the block starts drifting. It feels more like a useful training conversation and less like starting over in a blank chat.

It also sets up where we want to go next: real coaches and their runners working through the same plan, with simple delegated access to review and manage it together.
