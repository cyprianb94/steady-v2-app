02 · Export Configuration

  • Everything on by default. The physio needs the full picture — runners shouldn't have to guess what's relevant. A complete report is always more useful than a filtered one. Toggles exist for privacy ("I don't want to share effort ratings") not for curation.
  • 8 weeks is the sweet default. Most running injuries develop over 4–8 weeks. Four weeks might miss the onset; anything over 12 is noise. The 8-week window captures the acute-on-chronic pattern physios look for — recent escalation against a backdrop of building load.
  • "Include Steady's notes" is the trust-builder. It signals to the runner that Steady has been watching their patterns and can contribute something the raw data alone doesn't show. For the physio, these AI-generated observations ("load jumped 18% in week 3") are starting points for their own analysis.
  • Why this is a bottom sheet, not a full screen. The runner has already decided to share — they tapped the CTA. This step should feel quick and lightweight, not like filling out a form. Most people will just hit "Generate PDF" without changing anything. The sheet respects that.
  • Share methods row mirrors the native iOS/Android share sheet patterns runners already know. Email is the most common — physios rarely use apps for intake. AirDrop covers in-person appointments. "Link" generates a web-viewable version for physios who prefer not to open PDFs.
  • For the landing page: This screen shows how little friction there is between "I should tell my physio about this" and actually doing it. One tap to open, one tap to generate. The configuration exists but isn't required.
